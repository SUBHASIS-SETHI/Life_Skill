# Listening and Active Communication

## 1. Active Listening

### Question 1  
What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?

- Pay full attention  
- Maintain eye contact  
- Avoid interrupting  
- Observe body language  
- Ask small clarifying questions  
- Summarize what the speaker said  
- Respond calmly  
- Stay open-minded  

---

## 2. Reflective Listening

### Question 2  
According to Fisher’s model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

- Listen without judging  
- Repeat or rephrase what the speaker said  
- Show that you understand their feelings  
- Confirm if your understanding is correct  
- Keep your tone calm and respectful  
- Focus on the speaker, not your own thoughts  

---

## 3. Reflection

### Question 3  
What are the obstacles in your listening process?

- Getting distracted  
- Thinking about my reply instead of listening  
- Interrupting too early  
- Making assumptions  
- Losing focus in long conversations  

### Question 4  
What can you do to improve your listening?

- Practice staying present  
- Take short notes  
- Allow others to finish speaking  
- Ask simple clarifying questions  
- Avoid multitasking while listening  

---

## 4. Types of Communication

### Question 5  
When do you switch to Passive communication style?

- When I want to avoid conflict  
- When I feel unsure or nervous  
- When I don’t want to hurt someone’s feelings  

### Question 6  
When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles?

- When I feel stressed  
- When I think I am not being heard  
- When something happens repeatedly and frustrates me  

### Question 7  
When do you switch into Passive Aggressive communication styles?

- When I feel upset but don’t want to say it directly  
- When I feel ignored  
- When I expect others to understand without me saying it clearly  

### Question 8  
How can you make your communication assertive?

- Speak clearly without fear  
- Use “I” statements  
- Maintain a calm tone  
- Set boundaries respectfully  
- Say no politely when needed  
- Share feelings honestly instead of hiding them
